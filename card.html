Platypus

The platypus is one of the most unusual animals in the world.It has a bill like a duck, a tail like a beaver, and feet like an otter.It is one of the few mammals that lay eggs instead of giving birth to live young.

  • Scientific Name: Ornithorhynchus anatinus
  • Average Length: 50 centimeters
  • Average Lifespan: 6 to 15 years
    (up to 21 years in the wild)
  • Habitat: Freshwater rivers and lakes in eastern Australia and Tasmania

The platypus is a carnivore and feeds on insects, larvae, and small shrimp. It uses its soft, leathery bill to sense electrical signals from its prey underwater. Male platypuses have a venomous spur on their hind feet, which they use for defense. They are excellent swimmers and can stay underwater for up to two minutes.
